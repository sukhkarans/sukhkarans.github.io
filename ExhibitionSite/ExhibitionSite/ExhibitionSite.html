Did you know...

The average cost for an EV is $56,437. This high upfront cost makes it unaffordable for most current car buyers.

Source: CNBC

Currently in the US, it will take driving an EV about 15,000 miles, about a year of driving, to become cleaner than an equivalent gasoline car in terms of its lifetime carbon footprint.

Source: Reuters

Source: Investopedia

EVs costing under $50k qualify for up to $10k off your total price in discounts and tax credits between state and federal subsidies.

Source: AFDC.gov

Due to increased litigation surrounding vehicle emissions and a societal trend towards cleaner power sources, more and more vehicle manufacturers are starting to shut down their ICE vehcile production lines in favor of producing EVs.

Source: HotCars

Factoring in average fuel cost per mile for an ICE vehicle and comparing it the the average electricity cost per mile for an EV, the break-even point for an EV in fuel costs alone is at 300,000 miles. While this may seem high, EVs don't have nearly as many moving parts as ICE vehicles and don't require much maintenance throughout their lifespans, so they will last much longer than the typical ICE vehicle does.
